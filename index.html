Romanticism

History

Romanticism is a movement in art and literature that originated in the late 18th century. It characterized works of painting, music, and architecture. Romanticism was a reaction against Enlightment, 18th century rationalism and physical materialism in general. Its attitudes consisted of an appreciation of the beauty of nature, emotion over reason, senses over intellect, and an examination of human personality. This website focuses on the beautiful paintings and creative artists of the movement.

Source: Brittanica

Influence and Legacy

Romanticism originated in France and Great Brittain. It was influenced by and was a response to the disappointment with Enlightment values of reason and order tht originated after the French Revolution in 1789. Romanticism was also influenced by the industrial revolution, and it was often seen as an artist's way to escape modern reality. The movement was sometimes referred to as "Counter-Enlightment." It's legacy still lives on in society. Romanticism influenced political ideology and it brought focus to the poor and the oppressed, as well as generated ideals of social emancipation and progress. Just as important, it generated a sense of personal commitment to others and to society in general.

Source: Boise State University
